Spotlight on Community Library

Home Our Programs Spotlight on Community Library

Community libraries have emerged out of a deep felt need for libraries that are accessible to all sections of society, for books and learning to be held in collective ownership by the community that accesses the space, and out of a wish to see more libraries that are free, available to all and not gatekept by elite institutions or memberships.


In a country like India, given the steep dualism and inequalities between the affluent class/ privileged castes and the underprivileged that are starkly visible, the importance of community libraries is immense. For children from disadvantaged communities growing up in this context with a learning gap that is today further widened by unequal access to technology, open libraries act as catalysts in empowering the community and hold significant symbolic value for the marginalized.


The Spotlight on Community Libraries' project was born out of a need to highlight the various community libraries in function across India, and to document their varying processes, ideologies, goals and significance.


We wish to generate through this process, a method by which community libraries/free libraries can be systematically documented while also emphasising why such documentation is important, how it can be done, and the significance of a collective database that brings together a network of libraries doing community-based/community-driven work. We believe that showcasing and recording the activities, interventions and impact of free libraries will open a window towards understanding the nature of the free library movement in India.
